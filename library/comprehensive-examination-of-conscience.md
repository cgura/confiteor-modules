# Faith — January: Mortal Sins
- **Category:** COMPREHENSIVE_VIRTUES
- **Framework:** TEN_COMMANDMENTS
- **Examination Type:** COMPREHENSIVE
- **Month:** 1
- **Description:** Focuses on trusting God and believing His word, while avoiding superstition or despair. (CCC, 1840)

### 1

Have I denied that I was a Catholic?

> I denied my Catholic faith.

### 2

Have I openly expressed disbelief in any doctrine of the Catholic faith?

> I openly expressed disbelief in a doctrine of the faith.

### 3

Have I affiliated myself with a non-Catholic sect or religious body?

> I affiliated myself with a non-Catholic sect or religious body.

### 4

Have I suggested or encouraged doubts about the Catholic faith in the minds of others?

> I suggested or encouraged doubts about the faith in others.

### 5

Have I seriously expressed the opinion that all religions are equally good, equally true, or equally pleasing to God?

> I expressed the opinion that all religions are equally good or true.

### 6

Have I neglected to settle serious doubts about my faith through reading, study, or consultation, when such neglect was leading to a loss of faith?

> I neglected to settle serious doubts about my faith.

### 7

Have I read books that pretend to disprove the Catholic faith without proper reason?

> I read material intended to disprove the Catholic faith without proper reason.

### 8

Have I distributed books that pretend to disprove the Catholic faith?

> I distributed material intended to disprove the Catholic faith.

### 9

Have I, without a serious reason, associated with persons who tried to destroy my faith?

> I associated without good reason with persons who tried to destroy my faith.

### 10

Have I attended meetings or listened to speeches that I knew would destroy or seriously weaken my faith?

> I attended meetings or speeches I knew would weaken my faith.

### 11

Have I joined a secret society forbidden by the Church?

> I joined a secret society forbidden by the Church.

### 12

Have I contributed to the advancement of a non-Catholic religious sect or movement?

> I contributed to the advancement of a non-Catholic religious sect.

### 13

Have I consulted a fortune-teller in the serious belief that I could learn the future?

> I consulted a fortune-teller believing I could learn the future.

### 14

Have I attended a spiritualistic seance?

> I attended a spiritualistic seance.

### 15
- **Applicable To:** PARENT

Have I, without the necessary permission, sent my children to a non-Catholic school without making provision to safeguard their faith?

> I sent my children to a non-Catholic school without safeguarding their faith.

### 5

Have I been slow in trying to banish or overcome doubts against faith?

> I was slow in trying to overcome doubts against faith.

### 6

# Hope — February: Mortal Sins
- **Category:** COMPREHENSIVE_VIRTUES
- **Framework:** TEN_COMMANDMENTS
- **Examination Type:** COMPREHENSIVE
- **Month:** 2
- **Description:** Focuses on trusting that God will help you, forgive you, and lead you to eternal life. (CCC, 1840)

### 1

Have I denied the necessity of God's help for my salvation, believing I could win heaven by my own efforts alone?

> I denied the necessity of God's help for my salvation.

### 2

Have I said or seriously believed that, because God is so merciful, no one will be condemned to hell—and therefore I will surely be saved even though I have committed serious (mortal) sins?

> I presumed on God’s mercy, believing He would not condemn me and that I would surely be saved despite serious (mortal) sins.

### 3

Have I continued in mortal sin because I believed I would surely receive the grace to repent later?

> I continued in mortal sin, presuming that I would repent in the future.

### 4

Have I committed a mortal sin just because I expected God to forgive me in confession afterwards?

> I committed mortal sin expecting easy forgiveness in confession.

### 5

Have I neglected all prayer for periods of a month or longer?

> I neglected all prayer for a month or longer.

### 6

Have I refused to pray during a serious temptation because I did not want God to help me overcome it?

> I refused to pray during a serious temptation because I did not want God’s help to overcome it.

### 7

Have I deliberately put myself in a serious occasion of sin, assuming God would miraculously protect me—or forgive me—if I fell?

> I deliberately placed myself in a serious occasion of sin, trusting in God’s protection or forgiveness if I fell.

### 8

Have I said or thought that it was impossible for me to overcome a particular sinful habit?

> I despaired of overcoming a particular sinful habit.

### 9

Have I believed that because my past sins were so numerous or terrible, I could not expect God to forgive me?

> I despaired of God's forgiveness because of the severity of my sins.

### 10

Have I quit going to Mass because of the thought that it does no good?

> I quit going to Mass, thinking it did no good.

### 11

Have I quit praying because of the thought that it does no good?

> I quit praying, thinking it did no good.

### 1

> I deliberately entertained impure thoughts and images.

### 2

> I consented to impure desires without trying to resist.

### 3

Have I caused or consented to solitary sins of impurity (lust/masturbation) when I was alone?

### 11

# Meekness — November: Venial Sins
- **Category:** COMPREHENSIVE_VIRTUES
- **Framework:** TEN_COMMANDMENTS
- **Examination Type:** COMPREHENSIVE
- **Month:** 11
- **Description:** Focuses on patience and gentleness—responding calmly instead of getting angry or bitter.

### 1

Have I taken part in petty quarrels and bitter arguments?

> I took part in petty quarrels and arguments.

### 2

Have I given in to sudden spurts of anger by harsh words, calling names, or abusive language?

> I gave in to angry outbursts with harsh words.

### 3
Have I shown dislike and antipathy for others by snubbing them or being sarcastic?

> I showed dislike by snubbing them or being sarcastic.  

### 4
Have I shown sensitiveness and hurt feelings over trifling matters?

> I got upset or hurt feelings over small things that were not really important.

### 5

Have I teased others until I made them angry?

> I teased others until they became angry.

# Humility — December: Mortal Sins
- **Category:** COMPREHENSIVE_VIRTUES
- **Framework:** TEN_COMMANDMENTS
- **Examination Type:** COMPREHENSIVE
- **Month:** 12
- **Description:** Focuses on truth about yourself before God—avoiding pride and accepting correction.

### 1
Have I skipped Mass on Sunday without a good reason?

> I skipped Mass on Sunday without a good reason.  

### 2
Have I eaten meat on a day when the Church asks me to avoid meat?

> I ate meat on a day when I should have avoided it.  

### 3
Have I refused to fast on the days the Church asks me to fast?

> I refused to fast on the Church’s fasting days without a good reason.

### 4

Have I refused to forgive someone who wronged me because I considered my honor more important than God's command to forgive?

> I refused to forgive out of pride in my own honor.

### 5
Have I used unfair ways in business because I cared more about getting ahead than about obeying God?

> I was unfair in business because I was proud and wanted to get ahead instead of obeying God.   

### 6
Have I refused to obey my superiors because I thought my knowledge and importance meant I didn’t have to listen?

> I refused to obey authority because of pride.  

### 7
Have I failed to confess mortal sins because I thought they were my business only and not for the priest to hear?

> I did not confess mortal sins because of pride.

### 8

Have I maintained that it is unnecessary for a person to pray?

> I maintained that prayer was unnecessary.

# Humility — December: Venial Sins
- **Category:** COMPREHENSIVE_VIRTUES
- **Framework:** TEN_COMMANDMENTS
- **Examination Type:** COMPREHENSIVE
- **Month:** 12
- **Description:** Focuses on truth about yourself before God—avoiding pride and accepting correction.

### 1

Have I been guilty of vanity, considering myself more intelligent, learned, or charitable than others?

> I was vain, considering myself better than others.

### 2

Have I bragged about my accomplishments, virtues, or abilities?

> I bragged about my accomplishments and abilities.

### 3

Have I given in to anger because I thought myself better than others and that they should know better than to cross me?

> I became angry out of pride, thinking others should not cross me.

### 4

Have I looked down on others who were less wealthy, cultured, learned, or gifted than I?

> I looked down on those less fortunate than me.

### 5

Have I neglected daily prayer as if I were strong enough to get along without God’s help?

> I skipped my daily prayers because I was proud, and I thought I didn’t need God’s help.
